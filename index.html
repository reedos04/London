200AD

The defensive wall as known as the 'London Wall' was built. Still standing in certain parts today can easily be seen around the city!

1185

Temple Church, built for the Knights Templar as their English headquaters

1215

King John signs the Magnar Carta at Runnymede, 15th June 1215.

1666

The Great Fire of London, Sunday 2 September to Thursday 6th September gutted the city

1675

St Pauls Cathedral starts construction under the eye of Christopher Wren.

1842

Completion of the first 'subaqueous tunnel' under a navigable river system opened under the Thames, designed by Mark Isambard Brunel
using his patented tunneling shield , taking 18 years to complete.

1863

In 1863 the first underground station opened in London, the Metropolitan Line, and was built using the cut and cover technique
taking two years to complete.Harry Beck in 1933 designed the tube map, still in use today, based on an electrical circuit drawing

London!

London, the capital of the United Kingdom, was established by the ancient Romans in 47AD. One of the
oldest and most hisorically rich cities in the world, it's a mecca for a diverse range of individuals,
making it one of the most culturally populated cities in the world: with the majority of worlds nationalities
represented here. There is something for everyone to see and do, from the old heritage listed buildings/ structures
to the famous/infamous inhabitants (The Royal Family to Jack the Ripper ) to the new skyscrapers that
tower over the streets. With easy to use public transport (buses / the tube) and Santander docking bikes
navigating the streets to reach one tourist hotspot to the next makes visiting the city an expierence to
remember. If you are lucky and the weather behaves with blue skies and glorious sunshine you can expect
a many local to be at one Londons spacious parks (Hyde/Regents/Green/Greenwhich/Victoria/London Field) reading
a book or sun bathing while blowing the froth of a pint (drinking a beer) or the cliente of the pubs overflowing
onto the streets you will find the city alive with enthusiastic tourists keen to snap a seflie with Tower Bridge
or the London Eye, every corner here is a story tell. Come to London and write yours!
